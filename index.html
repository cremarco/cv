Research Profile

I am a research assistant at the University of Milano-Bicocca, Department of Informatics, Systems, and Communication (DISCo) in Italy. I hold a PhD in Computer Science from the same university (2020). During my doctoral studies, I spent six months at Bielefeld University (Germany) in 2019, working within the Semantic Computing Group. Prior to that, I earned both my Bachelor's and Master's degrees in Computer Science at the University of Milano-Bicocca.

My research interests include knowledge graph construction, data enrichment, and, more recently, the application of artificial intelligence techniques for building clinical decision support systems and predictive models in the field of mental health.

Over the years, I have actively contributed to the Semantic Web and AI communities by publishing scientific papers in reputable conferences and journals. My involvement in the academic community also includes serving on the program committee of several top-tier international conferences, where I have taken on roles as chair and reviewer.
